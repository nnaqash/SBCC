Scotland's Kiltwalk

Walking with a kilt on. A far distance, you walk with a kilt on a far distance. You wear a kilt and walk a far distance.

Scotland’s Kiltwalk 2023 will take place _____ in Glasgow*. All fundraising will once again be topped up by 50% thanks to The Hunter Foundation. *Subject to latest NHS and Government guidelines.

Registration is now officially open for Scotland’s Kiltwalk on _____!

Spaces are limited, so please sign up now to avoid disappointment.

There are two walk lengths to choose from – The Mighty Stride (16.5 miles) that leaves in the morning from Glasgow Green and finishes in Meadow Leisure Centre in Dumbarton or The Wee Wander (3.5 miles) that leaves from Glasgow Green in the afternoon and finishes at the Riverside Museum.

You must select your preferred start time during registration:

The Mighty Stride – Every half hour from 7.00am until 10.30am

The Wee Wander – Every half hour from 1.00pm until 2.30pm

Family Events

Family events allow the whole gang to come along and meet other families that have gone through similar experiences. Whether it is a party, a zoo visit or a bowling trip. Everyone is welcome! The SBCC knows it is important for burn survivors to feel they are not alone in facing day-to-day difficulties. Our aim is to get the children and their families together as much as possible so that they can share and relate their experiences.

It is also important to have fun and we combine the two by getting together throughout the year.
